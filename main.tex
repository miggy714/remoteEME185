\section{Abstract / Executive Summary}
\label{sec:abstract}

%--- BEGIN CONTENT ---

% PARGRAPH 1: Project objective and motivation
A variety of complex mechanical systems are dependent on electronic components and mechatronic designs requiring sensors,
microcontrollers, etc. Sandia National Laboratories (SNL) has tasked our senior design team, 
Noble Aggie Labs with designing a passive temperature compensation device (PTCD) for the purpose of a robust system that will
function without reliance on electricity or humans. The device must respond to ambient air temperature to precisely control the flow of
high-pressure gas. The device will adjust the effective orifice diameter according to the 
Governing linear equation:

\begin{equation}
    d_{\text{eff}} = A_1 (T - T_{\text{ref}}) + A_0
    \label{eq: required_relationship}
\end{equation}

Be advised, $A_1 = \num{-6.0e-5} \pm 5\%$ in /\degC{}, $A_0 = 0.01 \pm 5\%$ in, and
$T-{\text{ref}} = \SI{25}{\celsius}$. The device must operate between \SI{-60}{\celsius}
and \SI{+80}{\celsius}, withstand pressure differentials up to \SI{10000}{psi} during choked flow,
fit within a $(\SI{10}{\centi\meter})^3$ mechanical envelope, and be compatible with hydrogen gas. 

% PARAGRAPH 2: Solution and progress to date

Over the course of this project, our team curated five actuator concepts
and ten valve concepts. All design concepts were then systematic screened and
weighted with various scoring matrices (Report~1). We have since converged on a single
architecture consisting of three primary subsystems:
(1)~a \textbf{aluminum thermal expansion rod} a singular metallic rod that responds to ambient temperature changes. 
The aluminum rod will have linear piston displacement as a result of thermal exapnsion via the following equation:

\begin{equation}
    \Delta L = L_0\,\Delta T\, \alpha\
\end{equation}

(2)~A \textbf{compound spur-gear train} comprising of two rack gears and two coaxial pinion gears with a gear ratio of
4:1. This amplifies and transmits the piston's linear motion to the valve; and 
(3)~a \textbf{sliding-plate orifice valve} (''pizza cutter'' design), in which two flat plates, one fixed and one translating
contain a shaped hole with the overlap between the two holes defining the effective flow orifice. As the actuator extends with 
increasing temperature, the moving plate translates downwards. This reduces the overlap area and shriking $d_{\text{eff}}$ per 
the requirements. 

%--- END CONTENT ---
